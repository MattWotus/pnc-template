About the PAC

How PNC’s Political Action Committees Work

PNC has two primary PAC’s that support federal, state and local candidates. Funding decisions are made by our Government Affairs Directors, without regard to party affiliation, and based on rules and criteria established by PNC bylaws and the Steering Committee.

Any employee who is a member of a PNC PAC may request a contribution for an eligible candidate for office. Conversely, non-PNC employees, and employees who are not members of the PAC, may not request contributions from the PACs.

Employee participation is strictly voluntary and confidential. Contributions to the PACs are subject to applicable political contribution limits and are not tax deductible.

The PACs solicit and accept contributions - preferably via payroll deduction - from all active employees with officer titles. These include Officer, Assistant Vice President, Vice President, Senior Vice President, Executive Vice President, Vice Chairman, Managing Director and Director.

Employees in certain areas that conduct business with state and local governments will be solicited for the federal-only PAC to comply with so-called “pay to play” prohibitions of the Municipal Securities Rulemaking Board and the SEC.

All PAC receipts and contributions are reported to the appropriate federal and state elections commissions, where that information is available for public disclosure.

Why PNC’s Political Action Committees are Important

PNC as a company is prohibited under federal law and laws of many states from making political contributions, so we depend on the PACs to help elect candidates for public office who support public policies important to PNC and our various stakeholders.

Political Action Committees are non-partisan associations, which are funded by voluntary contributions from eligible employees. They are a legitimate and transparent means of providing contributions to candidates for political office, and expressing common interests with one voice.

Involvement in the political process is an important part of our efforts to grow our business, become more competitive in the financial services marketplace, support our customers, clients and partners and develop strong communities where we work and live.
